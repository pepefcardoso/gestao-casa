### Journey A: Shopping Day (Mobile - Expo)

> **Context:** You are walking around a custom furniture store.

1. You see a beautiful dining table for the living room.
2. You open the application on your phone ultra-fast.
3. On the Home screen, you click the "Rooms" tab, select "Living Room", and read the note you left there weeks ago: _"Maximum space for table: 1.60m x 0.90m"_.
4. You measure the table in the store, see that it fits, and request a quote from the salesperson: $R\$\,3,500$ split into $5\times$ on the credit card.
5. You open the expenses section, click "+", log it as an installment-based `Budget` of $5\times$ linked to the "Living Room", and go home to think about it.

### Journey B: Month-End Closing (Web - Next.js)

> **Context:** You sit at your computer on Sunday night to organize your bills.

1. You open the Web application in your browser.
2. The dashboard displays a wide view of the financial calendar for the next 6 months.
3. You locate that month's financing line and click "Pay Installment". The system marks it as completed and subtracts it from the outstanding balance.
4. You analyze the list of `Budgets` created on your mobile phone. You click on the "Dining Table" budget (which you decided to buy) and switch the toggle to `Confirmed`. The system automatically converts that budget into 5 actual expense installments in your cash flow for the upcoming months.
